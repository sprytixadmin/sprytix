Research Agencies

List of Research Agencies in USA

There are several government institutions in the United States that engage in carbon research and related environmental studies. These institutions focus on various aspects of carbon emissions, climate change, carbon capture, and sustainable energy.


Map

Logo Name Description State Map
NASA NASA’s Earth Science Division NASA’s Earth Science Division conducts research on various Earth systems, including the carbon cycle and its interactions with the atmosphere, oceans, and land. TX
NOAA NOAA’s Climate Program Office and Earth System Research Laboratory NOAA’s Climate Program Office and Earth System Research Laboratory are involved in carbon-related research, including monitoring carbon dioxide levels in the atmosphere and studying oceanic carbon cycling. MD
USGS United States Geological Survey USGS studies carbon emissions, carbon sequestration, and the impacts of climate change on ecosystems. They provide valuable data on land-use changes, carbon storage, and emissions. VA
EPA Environmental Protection Agency Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) conducts research on carbon emissions, greenhouse gases, and air quality. They also provide information on carbon footprints and sustainable practices. DC
NREL National Renewable Energy Laboratory National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL) focuses on renewable energy research, including carbon-neutral technologies and strategies to reduce carbon emissions from energy sources. CO
LBNL Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory (LBNL) conducts research on climate change, carbon capture, and energy-efficient technologies. They contribute to understanding the carbon cycle and its implications. CA
ORNL Oak Ridge National Laboratory Oak Ridge National Laboratory ORNL’s Climate Change Science Institute conducts research on climate modeling, carbon storage, and impacts on ecosystems. TN
NCAR National Center for Atmospheric Research National Center for Atmospheric Research (NCAR) studies atmospheric and climate science, including the carbon cycle, carbon dioxide levels, and their effects on weather and climate patterns. CO
USDA US Department of Agriculture Department of Agriculture USDA’s Agricultural Research Service (ARS) conducts research on carbon sequestration in soils, sustainable agriculture, and the impact of land use on carbon emissions. DC
